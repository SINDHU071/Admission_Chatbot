About SIGC

Our heritage, leadership & vision since 1984

Our Story

Shrimati Indira Gandhi College was founded in 1984 by Vidya Seva Ratnam K. Santhanam with a single-minded devotion to educate and empower women. It is one of the first two colleges in Tamil Nadu established exclusively for women, named after the former Indian Prime Minister Indira Gandhi — whose life-size statue marks the college entrance.

From a humble beginning of just 2 courses and 10 teachers, the institution has grown into one of the most important women's colleges in India, with over 43 programmes, hundreds of faculty members, and thousands of alumni across the globe.

The campus is historic ground — in 1934, Mahatma Gandhi visited this site (then National College High School), and a Peepal tree known as "The Gandhi Tree" commemorates the visit with plaques in English and Tamil.

Under the current leadership of Dr. P. Gajalakshmi (Principal) and successive able principals, the college has earned NAAC 'A' Grade accreditation for multiple cycles, ISO 9001:2015 certification, and numerous national recognitions.
